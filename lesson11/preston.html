Lightening Strike Energized Power Plant

Preston's power plant was hit by lightening.

A massive bolt of lightening hit the Preston, Idaho power plant filling up the station's energy coffers. During Wednesday's nights rainstorm, the lightening hit the exact spot of the power plants generator, filling it to capacity. The stunned power manager, John Electrica said, "I have never seen anything like this before, residents will have free power for months."

Although, no one say the exact bolt, residents nearby report the load boom and a fully lighted night sky. When the employees of Preston Energy Incorporated inspected the plant, there was evidence of sparking and only cosmetic burnt spots. One employee, Susan Lightfoot exclaimed, "With the recent power shortages, this is nothing short of a miracle."

Many national experts are claiming the event to be impossible. However, Electrica has confidentally invited all skeptics to the scene. "There is just no other explanation than the events that we have described. For now, we will just all give thanks for this blessed power supply."
