A Rich Heritage from our Forefathers

From the First Pioneers and Beyond

A fairly new city by anyone standards, Mapleton wasn’t incorporated until 1948. Originally the land was used by settlers and farmers for grazing and farming form around Utah County. In the 1850’s the land was surveyed for farming and irrigation. Irrigation was installed by a group of men shortly after. The first permanent settlers came in the 1870s. The area was originally called Union Bench. But was later changed to Mapleton in the early 1900s.

It was during the 1900s that many of the residents were not being heard by the city of Springville. Because they were further out, they had different needs and conditions. A petition was started to separate from Springville and start their own community. The city was named after a small grove of Maple trees at the base of Maple Canyon.

It has continued to grow and flourish over the years. The city has had it’s fair share of challenges throughout the state and nations history. With time, many of the farms have phased out. It is becoming much more metropolitan. Many people from all over are now settling in the city adding it to the new modern area.

Mapleton is a Great Place to Live

Today We are a Thriving Close Knit Community

Mapleton loves its rural charm which is fast disintegrating with all the farmland being used up for new home developments. Many new move people are moving in, including a healthy dose from California. The population is growing rapidly. During its last US census, the population was around 9,000, it is believed to be more around 20,000 now as we wait the results of the 2020 census.

The city is located about 8 miles from Provo, 5 miles from Spanish Fork, and 3.5 from Springville. One of its most charming features is the view of Maple Mountain, once called Sierra Bonita. The mountain offers a since of familiarity and comfort to its residents as well as wonderful seasonal views. The. City has a total of 6 parks so far in its 12 square mile radius. The residents are hoping for more soon.

The public school district in the area is the Nebo District, one of the best in the state for test scores. Along with a great school district, the city's schools also have consisitly ranked in the top schools in the state. They are always in the top 1%. There are many young children and invested residents in giving their young residents a quality education. Crime is low with superb leadership in the first responders in the city.

Development and Property Values Rising

We have only just begun

The future of Mapleton can only be surmised as with any other place. Current forecasts put property values homes in very high price ranges in 30 years. In fact, it is predicted that home values will rival the costs of homes in Silicone Valley. As people seek to put down roots these days, everyone wants to return to their place of heritage. Those of whom bought homes in the housing recession of 2011 and 2012 have seen their home values double and in some cases triple in just ten years.

New schools are being built to accomodate the growth. However, the commercial side and commerece development of Mapelton are sorely lacking leading to high tax rates for its residents. New people moving in want to keep the small town feel while long time residents are struggling to pay taxes.

Many of the city traditions will continue to happen. They are a favorite of not only Mapelton residents but many of the surrounding areas too. Fortunately for the city, many local businesses help promote and pay for activities.

City Demographics and More

Home of Mountain Views and Friendly People

  • Location: Mapleton is nestled at the base of Maple Mountain and Ether Peak in Utah County.
  • Setting: The city has a small town feel with parks and paths throughout.
  • Population: The last 2013 census placed Mapelton at about 8,800 residents. Since then there have been many new home developments.
  • Schools: There are 3 Elementary schools, a junior high, and a middle school in Mapleton.
  • Geographical Size and Elevation: The city is only 12.58 square miles at an elevation of 4724 feet.
